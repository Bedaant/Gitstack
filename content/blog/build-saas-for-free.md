# Build Your First SaaS for $0 Using Open Source

Every SaaS founder's nightmare: spending $500/month on tools before making a single dollar. Here's how to build a complete tech stack for exactly $0.

## The $0 Stack Blueprint

### Authentication: Keycloak or Supabase Auth
Instead of Auth0 ($25/month), use Keycloak or Supabase's built-in auth. Both support social logins, MFA, and user management.

### Database: PostgreSQL + Supabase
Self-hosted PostgreSQL is free forever. Supabase adds a generous free tier with real-time subscriptions.

### Backend: Directus or Strapi
Need a CMS + API? Directus and Strapi give you a full admin panel and REST/GraphQL APIs without writing backend code.

### Frontend: Next.js or Nuxt
React and Vue frameworks are free and deploy easily to Vercel or Netlify's free tiers.

### Payments: Stripe (pay-as-you-earn)
Stripe takes a percentage only when you make money. No monthly fees.

### Email: Listmonk or Mailtrain
Replace Mailchimp ($20/month) with Listmonk. Self-hosted, beautiful, and handles newsletters + transactional emails.

### Analytics: Plausible or Umami
Google Analytics is free but privacy-invasive. Plausible and Umami are lightweight, GDPR-compliant, and self-hostable.

### CRM: Twenty or ERPNext
Twenty is a modern open-source CRM. ERPNext handles CRM, inventory, accounting, and more.

## Deployment Strategy

Use Railway, Render, or Fly.io free tiers for hosting. As you grow, scale gradually.
